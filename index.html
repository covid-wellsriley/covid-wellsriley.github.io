Wells-Riley Simulation for Philips Air Disinfection Devices

Model Parameters

*This app uses the theoretical Wells-Riley model for infection risk assessment of airborne diseases in indoor spaces. While this model estimates the probability of infection, it does not guarantee infection risk reduction in real life applications. This model does not account for super-spreaders and immunocompromised people, it also does not consider how the risk of infection increases in closer proximity to an infected person. The Wells-Riley model assumes perfect mixing which assumes constant values for temperature, pressure, and concentration. Therefore, the Wells-Riley model and this app can only be used for estimation as the mathematical model cannot simulate real life scenarios.

About

The Wells-Riley model estimates the probability of infection of airborne diseases in an indoor space considering the ventilation of the room. Ventilation of indoor spaces is measured by air changes per hour (ACH), which is the number of times that the total air volume in a room is completely removed and replaced in one hour. The Wells-Riley model stipulates that by increasing the number of air changes per hour in a given room, the probability of infection can be lowered. Please refer to the graph. This app calculates the number of equivalent air changes (eACH) produced by Philips Air Disinfection Devices required for a desired estimated probability of infection. In addition, the time for 99.99% pathogen removal efficiency is included.
